Strategy Notes

  • Attack oriented, but be sure to have some poison down first so he can score.
  • Versatile attack and no defense means he needs some help staying alive.
  • Blatantly Cheating pairs well with Characters that rely on their Unique Cards like The Wizard.
Background

Who is the Barbarian?

An octogenarian legend with a rusty sword, a bad back, and a deep belief that every conversation is either a fight or soon to be one.

Origin Story

When he was young, the Barbarian fought for glory and gold. When he got glory and riches, he fought for the reputation necessary to keep them. Now no one will fight him, and he considers that unfair, maidenless, and ultimately pointless since he doesn't mind doing a bit of traveling.

He's fought dragons, demigods, and bureaucrats, and never lost a fight he cared about. As time wore on his odor grew to match his reputation and temperament, and a few of his dead ancestors(who still owed him a bit of money) managed to convince Death himself to leave the Barbarian be for the time being.

So he roams the city looking for adventure: a rival soon-to-be-in distress, a lady of questionable virtue, or a tavern that serves ale strong enough to subdue his scent long enough for him to sneak away.

Win & Unique

Win Condition

Score if the Barbarian plays an Attack card on top of an already Poisoned character during his turn

Overkill is his specialty. If the Barbarian can land a finishing blow on a weakened foe, all the better.

Unique Card

Blatantly Cheating → Discard to your Discard Pile.

  • Costs one of your 4 Discards to play.
  • Draw the Face Card of your choice from your Draw Pile.
  • Can only be used once per turn
