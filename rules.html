How to Play

Overview

Zzards is a tactical, turn-based card game about juggling priorities and odds. Each round, you’ll play cards onto Characters to help them meet their Win Conditions and sabotage the enemy to prevent them doing the same.

Setup

  • Each player has a team of two to four Characters.
  • Each Character has:
    • Two sets of five Base Function Cards (like Poison and Pilfer)
    • A Win Condition.
    • Three copies of their Unique Card.

Turn Structure

  • 1) Draw Player's Hand to Full: 2 Characters draw 5 cards, 3 Characters draw 7 cards, 4 Characters draw 10 cards.
  • 2) Play/Discard Cards: Play cards from your hand onto Characters (friend or enemy) and discard and redraw up to 4 cards.
  • 3) End turn: tally the total successful wins in your team, and let your opponent take their turn.
  • 4) Repeat x3: the total tally at the end of three rounds is the final score
What is a Win Condition?

Win Conditions

Each Character scores in a different way. Some score by not playing at all. Some exist to prevent others from scoring..

Your job is to build a round where your team scores more than the enemy, sometimes by helping your own Characters score, and sometimes by making sure theirs absolutely do not.

Scoring

At the end of the round, Characters check whether they meet their Win Conditions. If they do, they score. If not, they don’t. Many effects modify the odds of scoring rather than hard-stopping it.

  • Attack can prevent a Character from scoring.
  • Fortify counters Attack.
  • Poison reduces scoring odds by 50% until removed by Cure.

Targeting

  • Cards can be played on any Character, friend or foe.
  • Card text always wins if a rule seems unclear.

Card Types

  • Base Function Cards: The core actions (Attack, Fortify, Poison, Cure, Pilfer, Distract).
  • Unique Cards: Character-specific effects (Face/Unique).

Common Gotchas

  • Attack affects scoring, not card play.
  • Distract is bait. It’s meant to be stolen.
